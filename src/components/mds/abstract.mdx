# Abstract

The exponential expansion of context windows in LLMs has unlocked capabilities for long-document understanding but introduced severe bottlenecks in inference latency and information utilization. Existing compression methods often suffer from high training costs or semantic fragmentation due to aggressive token pruning.

In this paper, we propose BEAVER, a novel training-free framework that requires no additional parameter updates and directly leverages the pre-trained semantic space of the target LLM, shifting compression from linear token removal to structure-aware hierarchical selection. BEAVER maximizes hardware parallelism by mapping variable-length contexts into dense page-level tensors via dual-path pooling, and preserves discourse integrity through a hybrid planner combining semantic and lexical dual-branch selection with sentence smoothing.

Across four long-context benchmarks, BEAVER sets new training-free state of the art on the retrieval-heavy RULER (+19.1 average points over the strongest training-free baseline) and on L-Eval, ranks as the strongest training-free method on LongBench and ZeroSCROLLS, and reduces compression latency by 26.9x over LongLLMLingua at the 1M-token scale, processing one million tokens in 10.4 seconds and offering a practical solution for high-throughput deployment.
